```html
<h3>Why Choose Nusa Penida for Diving and Snorkelling</h3>
<p>What makes Nusa Penida so special from a diving point of view? Here are five main reasons it earns the title of one of the best dive destinations on Earth.</p>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Perfect year-round climate.</strong> Like all tropical areas, Nusa Penida has wet and dry seasons, yet both are suitable for diving. The average water temperature near the island is about +27°C and rarely drops below +16°C.</li>
  <li><strong>Exceptional water clarity.</strong> The clear coastal waters offer excellent visibility to divers and snorkellers, even during the wet season.</li>
  <li><strong>Rich coral reefs.</strong> Nusa Penida's reefs — both hard and soft corals — grow in spectacular formations and host extraordinary biodiversity.</li>
  <li><strong>Manta rays year-round.</strong> Nusa Penida is one of the only places on Earth where you can see manta rays throughout the entire year. Visit between July and October and you may also witness the migration of the famous mola-mola sunfish, alongside octopuses, bamboo sharks, dolphins, and over 500 fish and clam species.</li>
  <li><strong>Suitable for all levels.</strong> Both novice divers and professionals can enjoy spectacular underwater sites. Even children are welcome to snorkel in the calm coastal waters — making it an ideal family destination.</li>
</ol>
```

### content1 (Part 02 — Best Spots)

```html
<h3>Snorkelling in Nusa Penida: Top 5 Spots</h3>
<p>Clear waters and calm currents make snorkelling one of the most-loved activities on Nusa Penida. Here are the five best spots you should not miss.</p>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Crystal Bay</strong> — An iconic spot with crystal waters enclosing a long coral reef growing right from the shore. Swim above the reef at 5–8 meters depth, then enjoy the beautiful beach views after.</li>
  <li><strong>Manta Point</strong> — The #1 spot on the island. Swim with manta rays, dolphins, and sharks in a safe and relaxed environment. Perfect for novice snorkellers and kids, with a bottom depth of no more than 15 meters.</li>
  <li><strong>Gamat Bay</strong> — Calm, warm waters with the richest marine life. The ideal choice if you are just starting your snorkelling journey.</li>
  <li><strong>SD Point</strong> — A combo of diving and snorkelling in one place. Warmer water means you can swim here year-round.</li>
  <li><strong>Toyapakeh</strong> — The gateway to Nusa Penida. Most boats arrive at this harbour, and the coral reefs right off the dock offer an incredible first snorkelling experience.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Diving in Nusa Penida: Top 5 Sites</h3>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Manta Point</strong> — The undisputed staple for divers. Located on the southwest coast within the Nusa Penida Marine Protected Area, this site hosts manta rays, bamboo sharks, octopuses, and many reef fish species. Catching a "manta highway" during your dive is a once-in-a-lifetime experience.</li>
  <li><strong>Crystal Bay</strong> — The best spot to observe the iconic mola-mola sunfish, especially in July–October. A long, continuous coral reef runs from the shore to depth, and dolphins are spotted here year-round.</li>
  <li><strong>Mangrove</strong> — Located between Nusa Penida and Nusa Lembongan, this site is prized by advanced divers for exceptionally rich marine life. Note: strong currents are present — check conditions before diving.</li>
  <li><strong>PED</strong> — Famous for drift diving and turtles. Combine your dive with a visit to the sacred Ped Temple on shore for an unforgettable day.</li>
  <li><strong>SD Point</strong> — Slow unidirectional currents, a huge coral reef, and abundant fish species make this the best drift diving location on the island.</li>
</ol>
```

### content (Part 01 — Introduction & Climate + Wet Season)

```html
<p>Nusa Penida is often called "the Golden Egg of Bali" because of its beauty and authentic charm. Yet, like any place on Earth, it has specific climatic conditions that can either turn your visit into a magic fairy tale — or spoil your impressions entirely.</p>
<p>Before you plan your trip, let's learn about local weather, tourist seasons, and tips to help you enjoy every day on the island.</p>

<h3>Local Climate at a Glance</h3>
<ol>
  <li>Nusa Penida sits in the tropical belt, 12 km southeast of Bali. Its climate is similar to Bali but not identical.</li>
  <li>Like all tropical areas, it has two seasons: <strong>dry</strong> (April–October) with low precipitation, and <strong>wet</strong> (November–March) with heavy rains.</li>
  <li>Daytime temperatures stay between +29 and +35°C year-round. Nights are +25–30°C. No winter in the traditional sense.</li>
  <li>Humidity averages around 90% — lower than in Bali, but still something to prepare for.</li>
  <li>Water temperature rarely drops below +26°C, making it ideal for diving, snorkelling, and swimming all year.</li>
  <li>Maximum altitude is 524 m. The island is hilly — be ready for some effort on the trails.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Wet Season on Nusa Penida (November–March)</h3>
<p>During the wet season, heavy rains often arrive without warning — a sunny morning can turn into a downpour by afternoon. Water temperatures are actually higher during this period, but visibility in the sea drops significantly.</p>
<p>Why is wet season considered low season for tourism?</p>
<ul>
  <li>Peak humidity brings discomfort even on sunny days</li>
  <li>Low water visibility makes diving and snorkelling less rewarding</li>
  <li>The famous mola-mola sunfish only appears July–September (when cold tides arrive) — wet season is the worst time to spot them</li>
  <li>January and February are the rainiest months — best avoided</li>
</ul>
```

### content1 (Part 02 — Wet Season Tips & Dry Season)

```html
<h3>Tips for the Wet Season</h3>
<p>If you must visit during wet season, here's how to make the most of it:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Pack lightweight, breathable clothes — and multiple changes</li>
  <li>Bring waterproof bags and rain gear</li>
  <li>Sunscreen, mosquito spray, and a hat are essential</li>
  <li>Plan outdoor activities in the morning — afternoons are more likely to bring rain</li>
  <li>December is the best low-season option: fewer tourists, lower prices, and not yet at peak rain intensity</li>
</ul>

<h3>Dry Season on Nusa Penida (April–October)</h3>
<p>From April to October, Nusa Penida is at its most beautiful. Sunny days dominate, humidity decreases, and water clarity is at its peak. This is the best time for diving, snorkelling, and beach activities.</p>
<p>Notable highlights of the dry season:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>July–October:</strong> Mola-mola sunfish migration — the most sought-after wildlife experience on the island</li>
  <li><strong>Year-round:</strong> Manta rays at Manta Point</li>
  <li>Water visibility at maximum — you can see corals, fish, and marine life in stunning clarity</li>
  <li>Better for families, seniors, and those sensitive to humidity</li>
</ul>

<h3>Tips for the Dry Season</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Use sunscreen generously — UV radiation is intense</li>
  <li>Always check current conditions before diving — Nusa Penida sits in a dynamic oceanographic zone with unpredictable currents</li>
  <li>Book accommodation and tours in advance — high season is busy</li>
  <li>Some sites like Mangrove can have surprisingly cold water in July–August, even in dry season. Bring a wetsuit for deep dives</li>
</ul>
```
